# Personality Map Structure

Use this file when building, continuing, validating, or updating the 9-block Personality Map.

## Contents

- Universal Three-Layer Format
- Block 1: Core of Personality
- Block 2: Psycho-Energetic Profile
- Block 3: Living Fire Element
- Block 4: Psychotype and Scientific Models
- Block 5: Way of Action
- Block 6: Social Self
- Block 7: Experience and Capital
- Block 8: Future and Goals
- Block 9: Personality Metrics and Quality of Life

Every block must use the three-layer format:

1. `ESSENCE`: 3-5 first-person sentences that capture the block's key truth.
2. `CONTEXT`: concrete facts, stories, situations, decisions, consequences, and quotes without analysis.
3. `STRUCTURED SUBSECTIONS`: first-person expert synthesis, following the subsection order below, without repeating examples already placed in context.

Do not finalize a subsection without enough grounded material. If data is missing, mark it as an open loop and ask a precise question later.

## Block 1: Core of Personality

Task: collect the user's stable foundation: who they are at the core, where energy comes from, which values do not bend, what feels like self-betrayal, and which fears or shadow patterns shape choices.

### 1.1 Archetype and Natural Strategy

Collect:

- 3-5 dominant archetypes (Jung/Pearson) explaining energy profile and action strategy;
- "I am strong when..." and "It is difficult/dramatic for me when...";
- concrete life examples where these archetypes appeared.

Write:

- archetypes;
- strengths and vulnerabilities;
- anchor examples in context.

### 1.2 Worldview and Values

Collect:

- 10 fundamental values, using Schwartz and the user's own language;
- ranked importance;
- non-negotiable red lines;
- situations where values conflicted and real choices revealed what won.

Write:

- ranked values;
- unacceptable list;
- 2-3 situation-tests with conclusions.

### 1.3 Inner Drivers and Mission

Collect:

- energy triggers through SDT and user language;
- personal meaning: why the user gets up in the morning;
- social meaning: what value the user brings others;
- existential meaning through Frankl: why all this matters at a larger level;
- examples for each level.

Write:

- energy triggers;
- three levels of meaning with anchors.

### 1.4 Conscience and Inner Compass

Collect:

- how the user detects self-honesty vs self-deception;
- non-flexible moral rules;
- examples where the compass worked and where it failed.

Write:

- honesty-check algorithm;
- non-flexible rules;
- 2-3 examples of compass success/failure.

### 1.5 Shadows and Distortions

Collect:

- 3-5 real fears;
- recurring cognitive traps from CBT/ACT style thinking;
- defense mechanisms: avoidance, rationalization, aggression, numbness, etc.;
- anti-strategies for exiting each pattern.

Write:

- fears;
- cognitive traps with anchors;
- defense mechanisms;
- exit protocol.

## Block 2: Psycho-Energetic Profile

Task: map the user's energy: where they recharge, where they fade, energy cycle, nourishing and destructive environments, recovery practices, and people who act as energy sources or drains.

### 2.1 Energy Cycle

Collect:

- energy peaks by time, state, and conditions;
- energy drops and triggers;
- burnout markers: body, emotion, behavior.

Write:

- Flow / Execution / Recharge modes;
- conditions for transitions;
- state markers.

### 2.2 Energy Sources

Collect:

- people who charge or drain the user;
- places that charge or drain the user;
- activities that charge or drain the user.

Write:

- charge/drain table across people, places, and activities.

### 2.3 Harmful Environments

Collect:

- situations where the user loses energy, focus, or identity;
- toxic social types and how they drain resource;
- patterns that make the user enter or stay in these environments.

Write:

- energy protection protocol;
- harmful environments;
- red flags;
- exit/boundary algorithm.

### 2.4 Recovery Practices

Collect:

- quick practices for 10 minutes;
- medium practices for 1 hour;
- long reset for 1 day;
- body markers that signal the need to stop.

Write:

- recovery recipes by level;
- body fatigue markers.

## Block 3: Living Fire Element

Task: map aliveness and play: inner child, humor, speech, lightness, taste for life, where Fire burns and where it goes out.

### 3.1 Archetype of Aliveness

Collect:

- how the user plays life;
- what role appears when they are in their element.

Write:

- aliveness archetype;
- concrete examples.

### 3.2 Nature of Humor

Collect:

- humor style: irony, sarcasm, absurdity, dark humor, wordplay, etc.;
- what makes the user laugh;
- where humor is protection, weapon, or connection.

Write:

- humor profile;
- examples;
- functions of humor.

### 3.3 Speech Style

Collect through questions and observation:

- tempo, rhythm, signature words and phrases;
- directness vs metaphor;
- where speech is energetic vs dry.

Write:

- speech style;
- characteristic language patterns.

### 3.4 Emotion of Aliveness

Collect:

- dominant emotion when the user is in their element;
- how the emotion is lived;
- what turns it on and off.

Write:

- dominant aliveness emotion;
- activation and shutdown triggers.

### 3.5.1 Who Are Not My People

Collect:

- types of people with whom the user's Fire goes out;
- signs of non-resonance.

Write:

- "not my people" types;
- examples and signs.

### 3.5.2 Who Are My People

Collect:

- types of people with whom the user's Fire grows;
- signs of resonance, play, and courage.

Write:

- "my people" types;
- examples and signs.

### 3.6 Meaning Layer

Collect:

- themes, ideas, and directions of thought that feed aliveness.

Write:

- meaning zones where Fire burns;
- examples.

### 3.7 Body Layer

Collect:

- how aliveness appears in the body;
- sensations, movement, posture, energy.

Write:

- body markers of aliveness;
- examples.

### 3.8 Energy Layer

Collect:

- how energy changes when the user is in Fire: acceleration, expansion, intensity, clarity, etc.

Write:

- energetic profile of aliveness;
- patterns.

### 3.9 Fire Formula

Write the formula:

```text
My Fire burns when...
My Fire goes out when...
```

## Block 4: Psychotype and Scientific Models

Task: build a practical model profile so the Navigator can respond in a way that matches the user's psychology. Models are approximations, not labels.

### 4.1 MBTI / Cognitive Functions

Collect:

- closest MBTI working profile;
- dominant cognitive functions;
- where the profile works as strength;
- where it creates distortion or risk.

Write:

- closest profile and functions;
- strength/weakness;
- anchors and caveats.

### 4.2 Big Five + HEXACO

Collect:

- Openness;
- Conscientiousness;
- Extraversion;
- Agreeableness;
- Neuroticism;
- Honesty-Humility.

Write:

- high/medium/low for each;
- strengths, risks, anchors.

### 4.3 Enneagram

Collect:

- closest Enneagram type;
- wing;
- health level;
- strength and risk.

Write:

- closest type and wing;
- strength/weakness;
- anchors and caveats.

### 4.4 StrengthsFinder / Gallup

Collect:

- top 5 talents or recurring strengths;
- where they show most strongly;
- where they may be overused.

Write:

- top strengths;
- useful expression;
- overuse risk.

### 4.5 Attachment Style

Collect:

- closest attachment pattern;
- how it appears in romantic, friendship, and work relationships;
- where it gives strength and where it creates conflict.

Write:

- attachment working profile;
- relationship patterns;
- anchors and caveats.

### 4.6 Decision Style

Collect:

- System 1 vs System 2 tendency;
- context through Cynefin: simple, complicated, complex, chaotic, unclear;
- where the style works and where it fails.

Write:

- decision profile;
- contexts;
- strength/risk.

### 4.7 Hogan-Style Profile

Collect:

- light side: normal expression;
- dark side: stress expression;
- inner motivators.

Write:

- normal profile;
- stress profile;
- motivators;
- anchors and caveats.

## Block 5: Way of Action

Task: map how the user plays life: decision algorithm, work style, task style, reaction to mistakes and crises, start rituals, and failure protocol.

### 5.1 Decision Algorithm

Collect:

- intuition, logic, or mix;
- how the user checks decisions before final action;
- red flags that stop or delay "yes".

Write:

- decision scheme;
- 5 rule-checks before saying yes;
- red flags.

### 5.2 Task and Work Style

Collect:

- what produces flow;
- what causes rejection or drains motivation.

Write:

- top work modes;
- no-go list.

### 5.3 Mistakes, Stress, Crises

Collect:

- first reaction to mistakes;
- stress behavior;
- how the user returns from crisis.

Write:

- crisis protocol: first reaction -> what to do -> how to return -> recovery markers.

### 6.1 Communication Style

Write:

- influence style;
- listening style;
- conflict style;
- toxic triggers.

### 6.2 Leadership

Collect:

- management style;
- how the user grows people;
- how the user takes responsibility.

Write:

- 5 leadership principles;
- examples in context.

### 6.3 Interaction Strengths

Collect:

- networking style;
- charisma patterns;
- social capital strategy.

Write:

- social asset map.

## Block 7: Experience and Capital

Task: inventory what the user has accumulated: roles, hard/soft/meta skills, unique scaling zones, underestimated potential, and golden capital.

### 7.1 Past Roles

Collect:

- significant roles across work, projects, hobbies, volunteering, life;
- what the user learned in each;
- unique capital gained.

Write:

- role list;
- skills and insights;
- unique capital per role.

### 7.2 Skills

Collect:

- hard skills;
- soft skills;
- meta-skills.

Write:

- skill table with level: beginner, intermediate, advanced, expert;
- growth zone for each skill.

### 7.3 Unique Scaling Zones

Collect:

- where the user is unusually strong relative to their real context, only when supported by strong anchors;
- where the user plays too small or underuses potential.

Write:

- unique zones;
- underestimated potential;
- anchors.

## Block 8: Future and Goals

Task: map future direction: desired state, 3-10 year goals, anti-goals, failure scenarios, and chain from vision to milestones to daily rhythm.

### 8.1 Emotional Map of the Future

Collect:

- states the user wants to live in;
- desired impressions, tempo, environment;
- what "a good life" feels like.

Write:

- emotional future map;
- states, tempo, environment, images.

### 8.2 Goals for 3-10 Years

Collect:

- professional realization and finances;
- creative projects;
- personal life, health, growth, quality of life;
- social impact.

Write:

- goals by category;
- milestones for 3, 5, and 10 years when possible.

### 8.3 Anti-Life

Collect:

- what would feel like wasting life;
- future scenarios the user rejects;
- what the user is running from.

Write:

- anti-goals;
- rejected scenarios;
- avoidance patterns.

### Result of Block 8

Write:

- 10-year vision;
- milestones;
- daily rhythm that supports the vision.

## Block 9: Personality Metrics and Quality of Life

Task: create the user's self-diagnostic system: Health / Anxiety / Crisis indicators, body markers, failure protocol, turning points, and repeating patterns.

### 9.1 State Markers

Collect six categories:

- flow;
- energy level;
- sense of meaning;
- social interaction;
- material indicators;
- development speed.

Write:

- table with three levels: Health, Anxiety, Crisis.

### 9.2 Body Markers

Collect:

- body signals for health;
- body signals for anxiety;
- body signals for crisis.

Write:

- body marker list across three levels.

### 9.3 Failure Protocol

Collect:

- what to do in yellow-zone anxiety;
- what to do in red-zone crisis;
- proven actions that return the user to normal.

Write:

- Anxiety -> action;
- Crisis -> action;
- Return to normal -> algorithm.

### 9.4 Turning Points and Repeating Patterns

Collect:

- life turning points;
- crises, breakthroughs, key decisions;
- repeating patterns and mistakes;
- what helped the user exit patterns before.

Write:

- turning point list;
- repeating pattern map;
- proven exits.
